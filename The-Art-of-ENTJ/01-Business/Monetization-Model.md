# Monetization Model — the five streams

$30k/month is a **stack**, not a single source. Each stream turns the same audience into revenue at a different point in their relationship with the channel. Build them roughly in this order — each one funds and feeds the next.

## Stream 1 — AdSense (the floor)

The baseline. Automatic once you're in the [Partner Program](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/72851). No selling required.

- **Driver:** monetized views × RPM.
- **RPM planning number:** $12 blended (this niche, tier-1 audience). Validate against your own analytics after 90 monetized days.
- **Levers that raise it:** longer videos (8+ min unlocks mid-roll ads), tier-1 geography (your audience already skews US/UK/CA/AU), advertiser-friendly topics (strategy/psychology is safe; avoid content that trips [limited ads](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/6162278)).
- **At 1M views/month:** ~$12,000. Reliable but algorithm-dependent and seasonal.

## Stream 2 — Sponsorships (the multiplier)

Brand deals are the fastest revenue-per-view once you have ~20k+ engaged subscribers. Your audience — ambitious, mid-career, high-intent — is exactly who productivity, finance, education, and B2B-adjacent sponsors pay premium CPMs to reach.

- **Pricing:** integrated 60–90s reads typically sell at a **$20–40 CPM on the video's expected 30-day views** — often more for a high-value B2B audience. A video expected to do 100k views can command $2,000–4,000.
- **Natural fits for this brand:** book summary apps (Shortform, Blinkist), online learning (courses, language apps), finance/investing tools, note-taking/second-brain apps, VPN/privacy, career and negotiation platforms.
- **Rule:** the read must survive the brand's voice test. No hype, no "insane deal," no fear. A sponsor that forces a hyped read is the wrong sponsor — declining protects brand rule #3 (calm is the brand).
- **At maturity:** 2–3 integrations/month ≈ **$4,000–8,000**. Use marketplaces (e.g. YouTube [BrandConnect](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/9314941), or agencies) once inbound isn't enough.

## Stream 3 — Own product (the margin)

**This is the line that actually gets you to $30k.** Ads and sponsors are rented audience; a product is owned revenue with 80–95% margins and no algorithm between you and the sale. The brand is uniquely suited to this because it already promises "so what do I do" — a product is just the fuller answer.

Product ladder, cheapest to build first:

1. **Paid community / membership** (Skool, Patreon, or YouTube [channel memberships](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/7636690)). Monthly recurring. Price $15–30/mo. *This is the flagship.* At $19/mo, **~530 members = $10,000/month recurring.** Deliver: monthly "operator's briefing," breakdowns too spicy for public, templates, and a peer network of ambitious people — the community is half the value.
2. **The Playbook** — a $49–99 self-paced product that packages the channel's frameworks (the headline engine, the strategic-literacy lessons) into a structured course or a premium PDF/Notion system. Sells passively off every video.
3. **Cohort or premium tier** — a $300–1,000 higher-touch offer once you have proof and testimonials. Optional; high effort.

**Guardrail (brand rule #2 and the honesty gate):** never sell in the first 10 seconds, never fear-sell, and never promise the video can't cash. The product is mentioned at the *end*, as the deeper version of the free lesson. Trust is the funnel.

## Stream 4 — Affiliates (the passive layer)

Recommend what you genuinely use and cite. Books are the obvious, on-brand fit (Machiavelli, Greene, negotiation and game-theory titles) via [Amazon Associates](https://affiliate-program.amazon.com/); plus the tools you actually use.

- **Placement:** pinned comment + description, never mid-narration.
- **At scale:** **$1,000–3,000/month.** Modest, but it's free money on content you're publishing anyway.

## Stream 5 — Licensing / expansion (later, optional)

Once the machine runs: a second channel (the Brand Bible already names *Dark Motives 101* as sibling positioning), a newsletter with its own sponsorships, or a book. Don't build these until Streams 1–3 are humming. Focus is the moat.

## The blend, at three sizes

| Monthly views | AdSense | Sponsors | Product | Affiliates | **Total** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 300,000 | $3,600 | $1,500 | $3,000 | $500 | **~$8,600** |
| 1,000,000 | $12,000 | $6,000 | $10,000 | $2,000 | **~$30,000** |
| 2,000,000 | $24,000 | $12,000 | $16,000 | $3,000 | **~$55,000** |

**Read the middle row as the $30k target.** Note that even at 1M views, the *product* line is a third of revenue — that's the whole thesis. A creator who only chases views needs ~2.5M/month to hit the same number. Build the audience *and* the offer.
