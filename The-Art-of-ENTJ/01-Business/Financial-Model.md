# Financial Model — costs, unit economics, and profit

Revenue is only half the picture. This is what it costs to run the machine and what's actually left. Numbers are planning estimates in USD — replace them with your real vendor quotes as you go.

## Cost per video

The faceless model's advantage is a low, fixed marginal cost per upload. Two configurations:

| Line item | DIY (you edit) | Outsourced (freelance editor) |
|---|---|---|
| AI voiceover (per video) | $3–8 | $3–8 |
| AI/stock visuals, B-roll, music | $5–15 | $5–15 |
| Editing | your time (~4–6 hrs) | $80–200 |
| Thumbnail | your time (~1 hr) | $15–40 (or DIY) |
| Scripting | your time (~3–5 hrs) | $50–150 (or DIY) |
| **Cash cost per video** | **~$10–25** | **~$150–400** |

The strategic move: **DIY until revenue exists, then buy back your time.** Your first constraint is cash; your later constraint is hours. Outsource editing first (biggest time sink), scripting last (it's the brand's soul — keep control longest).

## Monthly operating costs

| Tool / service | Purpose | ~Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| AI voice (ElevenLabs or similar) | Narration, fixed voice profile | $22–99 |
| Research / SEO (vidIQ, TubeBuddy) | Topic + keyword intelligence | $10–40 |
| AI image/video generation | Visuals in the brand's dark style | $20–60 |
| Editing software | Assembly | $0–55 |
| Stock/music subscription | B-roll, licensed audio | $15–50 |
| Design tool (Canva/Figma) | Thumbnail templates | $0–15 |
| Community platform (Skool/Patreon) | Product hosting | $0–99 |
| **Total** | | **~$70–470/mo** |

Start at the low end. Add tools only when a bottleneck justifies them.

## Startup budget (first 6 months, pre-revenue)

| | Low | High |
|---|---|---|
| Tools (6 mo) | $420 | $2,800 |
| Voice model setup / testing | $50 | $200 |
| Freelance help (optional early) | $0 | $1,500 |
| Buffer | $200 | $500 |
| **Total** | **~$670** | **~$5,000** |

Plan realistically for **~$1,500–4,000**. You will earn **$0 from ads** until you clear the [Partner Program gate](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/72851) (1,000 subs + 4,000 watch hours). Product and affiliate revenue *can* start before that — a reason to launch at least a simple affiliate setup and a waitlist early.

## Profit at the $30k stage

At ~1M views/month with an outsourced pipeline running ~12–16 videos/month:

| | Monthly |
|---|---|
| Revenue (blended) | ~$30,000 |
| Production (14 videos × ~$300 outsourced) | –$4,200 |
| Tools & platforms | –$400 |
| Sponsorship/affiliate fees & misc | –$400 |
| Community/product delivery cost | –$500 |
| **Operating profit** | **~$24,500/mo (~80% margin)** |

That margin is the point of the faceless model: no studio, no on-camera talent, costs that barely move as views scale. The main input that *does* scale is the product/community — but that's high-margin too.

## Break-even and cadence math

- **Break-even on cash** happens the month ad + product revenue exceeds ~$400–500 in tooling. Realistically 4–9 months in.
- **Videos to critical mass:** most channels need **30–50 videos** before the algorithm has enough signal to push a breakout. At 2–3/week that's **~4–6 months of consistent output** before you should judge whether the concept works. Budget to survive that long — it's the [[Reality-Check|valley of death]].

## Three scenarios

| | Conservative | Base | Aggressive |
|---|---|---|---|
| Time to 1,000 subs | 6–9 mo | 3–5 mo | 6–10 wks |
| Time to Partner Program | 9–14 mo | 5–8 mo | 3–5 mo |
| Monthly views @ month 18 | 300k | 1M | 2M+ |
| Monthly revenue @ month 18 | ~$8k | ~$30k | ~$55k+ |
| Assumed cadence | 1–2/wk | 2–3/wk | 3–4/wk + Shorts |

**Base case is the plan of record.** Conservative is what you budget for. Aggressive is what you don't count on but stay ready for — a single breakout video can pull the whole timeline forward, which is why every upload runs the three-concept packaging discipline.
