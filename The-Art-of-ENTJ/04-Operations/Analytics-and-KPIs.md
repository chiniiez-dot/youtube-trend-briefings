# Analytics & KPIs — the numbers that matter

Decisions run on data, not memory (Brand Bible: performance logging). Track a fixed set of numbers per video, review them monthly, and turn every video into evidence. This page defines *which* numbers, *what they mean*, and *what to ignore*.

## The two numbers that decide reach

Before subscribers, before revenue, these two govern whether a video gets pushed:

1. **Click-through rate (CTR).** How compelling the package is. **Healthy: 4–10%** for browse/suggested. Below ~3% consistently → your [[Packaging-Playbook|titles/thumbnails]] are the bottleneck, not your content.
2. **Average view duration (AVD) / % viewed.** How well the video delivers. **Healthy: 40%+ average viewed** on 8–15 min videos. A strong hook + honest payoff drives this.

**CTR gets them in; AVD keeps them and tells YouTube to show more.** A video needs both. High CTR + low AVD = clickbait the algorithm will stop promoting (and the honesty gate should have caught).

## Per-video log (every upload)

Log these in the [[Video-Production-Note]] and a master sheet:

- Title + which [[Packaging-Playbook|headline structure]] it used.
- Thumbnail template (A–E) + winning concept (safe / tension / wildcard).
- **CTR at 48h and at 7 days.**
- **AVD and % viewed.**
- **Retention shape** — where the first big drop happens (the hook? a slow middle?).
- **Traffic source split** — browse / search / suggested.
- Pillar (MR / NP / CS / OM).

## Channel-level KPIs by phase

| Phase | Primary KPI | Target signal |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Prove | CTR + AVD | ≥1 video with CTR >5% *and* AVD >40% |
| 2 — Monetize | Watch hours + subs | 4,000 hrs / 1,000 subs |
| 3 — Business | Revenue mix + email/community signups | product line exists, revenue ≥ $8k/mo |
| 4 — Scale | Monthly views + MRR | ~1M views/mo, ~500 paying members |

## Revenue KPIs (Phase 3+)

- **RPM** (revenue per 1,000 views) — validate the $12 planning assumption against reality; it drives every projection.
- **MRR** (monthly recurring revenue from the community) — the $30k lever. Track members, churn, and new signups per video.
- **Sponsorship pipeline** — deals booked, CPM achieved, inbound vs. outbound.
- **Affiliate conversion** — clicks and sales per video.
- **Revenue per video** — total blended revenue ÷ videos published. The number that tells you if scaling output pays.

## Monthly review (turn the log into changes)

The engine of continuous improvement. Once a month:

1. **Rank** the month's videos by CTR and by retention *separately*. The top and bottom of each list are the lessons.
2. **Ask two questions.** What did the top performers share? What did the bottom share? Look at title structure, thumbnail template, and pillar.
3. **Form one hypothesis.** A single testable change. *"Tension-play thumbnails beat safe reads for concept videos — test more next month."*
4. **Test it** next month and log whether it held.
5. **Promote to the bible.** When a hypothesis holds two months running, write it into the [[Brand-Bible]].

## Vanity metrics to ignore

- **Subscriber count as a goal.** It's a lagging output of good CTR+AVD, not an input. Chasing it directly wastes effort.
- **Total lifetime views.** Feels good, decides nothing.
- **Single-video virality.** One spike isn't a business. The $30k gate in the [[Launch-Roadmap]] requires *sustained* revenue across two months precisely to filter out flukes.
- **Likes/comments in isolation.** Useful as engagement texture, not as a steering signal.

Keep it all in **one sheet**. The point is pattern detection *across* videos, not vanity on any single one.
