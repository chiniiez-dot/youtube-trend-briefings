# Risk & Compliance — how not to die

A faceless channel can be killed by things a personal channel never worries about. This is the list of what actually ends channels in this niche, and the standing rules that keep this one alive. None of this is legal advice — when in doubt, read YouTube's own policies (linked) or consult a professional.

## The existential risks

### 1. "Reused / repetitious content" demonetization

YouTube's [policy](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/6162278) demotes and demonetizes channels that mass-produce low-effort, templated, or AI-generated content with no meaningful original contribution. This is the #1 killer of faceless channels.

- **Your moat:** the **script is genuinely original strategic analysis in a distinct voice.** AI assists voice and visuals; it never authors the ideas. This is not a slop channel that reads a Wikipedia article over stock footage — it's original commentary. Keep it that way and you're firmly inside policy.
- **Standing rule:** every video must clear "would a smart person learn something they couldn't get from the source text alone?" If no, don't publish it.

### 2. Copyright / fair use

- **Music:** licensed or royalty-free only. No exceptions. A single claim can demonetize a video; a pattern can strike the channel.
- **Footage/images:** licensed stock or AI-generated. Don't lift other creators' clips.
- **Quoting texts** (*The Prince*, etc.): brief quotes for commentary are standard, but the value must be *your* analysis, not the excerpt. *The Prince* itself is public domain; modern translations and other authors' books are not — quote briefly and transform.

### 3. Single points of failure

- **One platform.** The whole business sits on YouTube's algorithm and policies. **Mitigate by owning the audience**: build the email list / community from Phase 2. If YouTube changes the rules overnight, the [[Monetization-Model|product line]] and email list survive. This is a business reason, not just a monetization one.
- **One voice vendor.** Keep your voice profile's source settings documented so you could rebuild it elsewhere if a vendor disappears or changes terms.
- **One income stream.** The entire [[Monetization-Model]] thesis — five streams — is itself risk mitigation. Ad-only channels die when RPMs drop; stacked channels don't.

## Policy & disclosure hygiene

- **Sponsorships:** disclose paid promotions (check the "includes paid promotion" box + say it in the video). Required by [YouTube](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/154235) and the [FTC](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/disclosures-101-social-media-influencers).
- **Affiliates:** disclose affiliate links in the description (FTC + Amazon Associates terms).
- **AI content:** disclose synthetic/altered media where YouTube's [rules](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/14328491) require it. AI *narration of your own writing* over illustrative visuals is generally low-risk, but stay current as policy evolves.
- **No misinformation, no harmful "manipulation how-to."** The brand teaches *strategic literacy* — reading power, not defrauding people. Keep it on the "understand the game" side of the line, never operational instructions for harming specific people. This is both an ethics line and a policy line.

## Brand-integrity risks (slower deaths)

- **Voice drift.** As you outsource, the channel can slowly stop sounding like itself. The [[Publishing-Checklist|checklist]] and written specs are the defense — the spec carries the taste, not the freelancer.
- **Monetizing too hard, too early.** Selling before trust is built poisons retention (brand rule #5). Product pushes wait until Phase 3; even then, soft and end-of-video only.
- **Clickbait creep.** The pressure to juice CTR erodes the honesty gate over time. The rule that a payoff-honesty score below 3 kills the video, no matter its numbers, is non-negotiable for exactly this reason.

## Business & financial hygiene

- **Separate finances** — a dedicated account for channel income/expenses from day one makes taxes and tracking sane.
- **Track deductible costs** — tools, freelancers, and software are business expenses. Keep receipts.
- **Contracts for sponsorships** — even a simple written scope (deliverable, usage, payment terms, exclusivity window) prevents disputes.
- **Diversify before you depend** — don't quit a day job on three good months. The [[Launch-Roadmap]] gate for stability is *sustained* revenue, not a spike.

## The standing rules (pin these)

1. Original analysis every video — the anti-slop moat.
2. Licensed assets only — no copyright roulette.
3. Own the audience (email/community) from Phase 2 — platform independence.
4. Disclose everything — sponsors, affiliates, AI.
5. Never sell before trust — protect the funnel.
6. The honesty gate is absolute — no video overrides it.
