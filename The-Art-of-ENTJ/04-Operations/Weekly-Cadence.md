# Weekly Cadence — the repeatable production week

The system that turns strategy into published videos on schedule, without quality drift or burnout. Batch by *stage*, not by video — doing all the scripting, then all the voiceovers, etc., is far faster than making one video end-to-end at a time.

## The batched week (2 videos/week example)

| Day | Block | Work |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Plan + Script | Pick 2 titles from [[Video-Backlog]]; run [[Packaging-Playbook|three-concept]] packaging; write both scripts to [[Script-Template]]. |
| **Tue** | Voice + Visuals | Generate both voiceovers (locked profile); assign and gather visuals per beat. |
| **Wed** | Edit A | Cut video 1 to the motion rules; audio + color pass. |
| **Thu** | Edit B + Package | Cut video 2; build both thumbnails; finalize titles. |
| **Fri** | Ship + Review | Run [[Publishing-Checklist]] on both; schedule to fixed slots; log baselines; refill backlog to 20+. |
| **Sat/Sun** | Off / buffer | Rest, or research next week's topics. Protect this. |

Scale to 3/week by adding a second edit day or outsourcing edits (Phase 3+). Never scale by skipping the checklist.

## Batching principles

- **One stage, many videos.** Context-switching between scripting and editing is the biggest hidden time cost. Stay in one mode per block.
- **Stay 1–2 weeks ahead.** Publish from a buffer, never live from today's work. A buffer is what lets you keep cadence through a sick week — and keeping cadence is the whole game.
- **The backlog never empties.** Refill it every Friday. A blank backlog on Monday costs you the week.

## Monthly rhythm

- **Weekly:** produce + ship on cadence; log every video.
- **Monthly:** run the [[Analytics-and-KPIs#Monthly review|monthly review]] — rank the month's videos by CTR and retention, ask what the top and bottom performers share, form **one** testable hypothesis, apply it next month.
- **Quarterly:** revisit the [[Launch-Roadmap]] gates. Are you where the phase says you should be? Adjust cadence, outsourcing, or monetization timing accordingly.
- **When a hypothesis proves out over two months:** write it into the [[Brand-Bible]]. The bible is a living file that gets sharper as the data comes in.

## Guardrails against burnout (this is a 24-month plan)

- The batch exists so no single day is overwhelming. If a day blows up, the buffer absorbs it.
- Outsource in order of pain: **editing first**, then thumbnails/research, then — last and reluctantly — scripting. Money buys back hours once revenue exists (see [[Financial-Model]]).
- Cadence you can sustain beats cadence that looks impressive for six weeks and then collapses. Pick a number you can hit on your *worst* week, not your best.
