# Script Template — the retention-engineered structure

Every script fills this shape. It's the Brand Bible's retention structure made concrete: open the curiosity gap fast, deliver the mechanism, close the gap, land "so what do I do." Copy the block at the bottom into each new [[Video-Production-Note]].

## The five movements

### 1. The Hook (0:00–0:15) — open the gap

The most important 15 seconds. Name the dynamic the viewer has felt but never articulated, or state the counterintuitive claim you'll owe an explanation for. **No throat-clearing, no "in this video," no intro animation first.** Start on the tension.

- Voice: statement, not a question. *"Power flows to whoever controls the exit."*
- Use one of the four gap types: named pattern · hidden mechanism · counterintuitive claim · insider frame.
- Second person for stakes: make it about *them*.

### 2. The Frame (0:15–1:30) — promise the payoff

Tell them the shape of what's coming without giving it away. Establish why this matters to their actual life. Introduce the anchor if it's a Machiavelli Read. This is where you earn the next eight minutes.

- Concrete over abstract. Name the mechanism you're about to dissect.
- Plant the stakes: what changes for them once they understand this.

### 3. The Mechanism (1:30–6:00) — deliver the goods

The body. Explain how the thing actually works, step by step. This is the "teach the mechanism, not the moral" core. Use the case study or archetype to make it concrete. Third person for analysis, second person when the lesson turns personal.

- One idea, developed fully. Don't sprawl.
- Every abstract claim gets a concrete instance. *"He made himself the only person who understood the system"* beats *"he became indispensable."*
- No hype adjectives. The idea carries the weight.

### 4. The Close (6:00–7:30) — pay off the gap

Explicitly close the curiosity gap you opened in the hook. The viewer should feel the click of *"so that's what that was."* A gap that pays off builds trust; one that doesn't trains them to stop clicking.

### 5. The Application (7:30–end) — "so what do I do"

Every video answers this or it's a lecture, not a manual (brand rule #4). Give one or two concrete moves the viewer can make tomorrow. Then — and only here — the soft product mention: the deeper version of this lesson lives in [the community/Playbook]. Never sell before this point.

- End cool. No "smash like." A calm, confident close is the brand's flex.
- End screen: point to the next logical video in the series.

## Voice checklist (run on the draft)

- [ ] Statements, not questions posing as insight.
- [ ] No hype adjectives ("insane," "shocking," "crazy"). If a claim needs them, the claim is weak.
- [ ] Concrete mechanisms named, not abstractions gestured at.
- [ ] Assumes intelligence — nothing obvious explained twice.
- [ ] Cool over hot. Restraint throughout.
- [ ] Reads like a field manual that happens to be about human beings.

```
TITLE:
PILLAR:            (MR / NP / CS / OM)
THUMBNAIL TEMPLATE: (A–E)
TARGET LENGTH:     (8–15 min)

[HOOK 0:00–0:15]
> open the gap. one strong statement.
  VISUAL:

[FRAME 0:15–1:30]
> promise the payoff, plant the stakes.
  VISUAL:

[MECHANISM 1:30–6:00]
> how it actually works. concrete instances.
  VISUAL:

[CLOSE 6:00–7:30]
> pay off the gap. the "so that's what that was."
  VISUAL:

[APPLICATION 7:30–end]
> one or two moves for tomorrow. soft product mention.
  VISUAL:
  END SCREEN → next video:
```
