# Content Strategy — pillars, cadence, and the slate

The Brand Bible defines *how* a video sounds and looks. This defines *what* to make and *how often*, so the channel builds a coherent body of work instead of 100 disconnected uploads.

## Content pillars

Every video belongs to one of four pillars. The mix keeps the channel varied without drifting off-brand. Rough target ratio in parentheses.

1. **The Machiavelli Read (30%)** — apply *The Prince* directly to modern life. The anchor. Names the house philosopher and keeps the channel coherent. *"Machiavelli said it's safer to be feared. He was half right."*
2. **The Named Pattern (30%)** — give a familiar, unspoken dynamic a precise name the viewer can use tomorrow. The channel's highest-retention format. *"The competence trap: why being the best gets you stuck."*
3. **The Case Study (20%)** — a historical or archetypal figure or event read through the strategy lens. Narrative pull + concrete mechanism. *"How one advisor made an entire court depend on him."*
4. **The Operator's Manual (20%)** — direct, tactical "so what do I do" videos on a specific skill: leverage, timing, negotiation, positioning. Closest to the product; strongest funnel to it.

Keep the anchor (Pillar 1) *felt across the channel*, not crammed into every upload — Brand Bible guidance: roughly one title in three names Machiavelli or *The Prince* directly.

## Cadence

- **Base plan: 2–3 long-form videos/week** (8–15 min), fixed publish days. Pick the days now and never move them — the algorithm and the audience both reward predictability.
- **Shorts: optional, 2–4/week** in Phase 2+ purely as a subscriber on-ramp that points to the long-form. Never the main product.
- **Length target:** 8–15 min. Long enough for mid-roll ads and real depth; short enough to hold AVD. Let the idea set the length — never pad to hit a runtime.

## Series design

Series create bingeable structure, boost session time, and give the audience appointment viewing. Launch 2–3 named series and slot most videos into one:

- **"The Prince, Decoded"** — one chapter/idea of *The Prince* per episode, applied to now. Directly serves the anchor and is a natural playlist + eventual product spine.
- **"Named"** — the pattern-naming series. Each episode installs one precise term into the viewer's vocabulary. High shareability ("there's a name for this").
- **"The Operator"** — tactical skill series. Feeds the product funnel most directly.

Series get their own [[Packaging-Playbook|thumbnail treatment]] (consistent template per series) so the audience recognizes the format before reading a word.

## Topic selection filter

Before a backlog idea becomes a production, it must pass all three (from the Brand Bible's selection stage):

1. **Does it name a pattern the audience is actually living?** If they haven't felt it, they won't click.
2. **Can it be delivered in the channel's frame** — calm, structural, "so what do I do"?
3. **Does it balance the slate?** Keep the pillar ratio roughly intact across each month.

## Evergreen over trend

This niche is **evergreen by design** — Machiavelli isn't a news cycle. That's a strategic advantage: videos keep earning views (and ad + affiliate + product revenue) for years, unlike trend-chasing channels that reset to zero every week. Prioritize titles that will make as much sense in three years as today. Use trends only as an occasional on-ramp to an evergreen lesson, never as the whole video.

## Where topics come from

- The [[Video-Backlog]] (maintained at 20+ ideas at all times).
- Comments — the audience will name patterns they want explained. Mine them.
- Keyword/opportunity research (vidIQ/TubeBuddy) for demand validation on the named-pattern terms.
- The source texts themselves: *The Prince*, Greene, game theory, negotiation literature. Every chapter is a potential episode.
