# Packaging Playbook — earning the click, honestly

The title and thumbnail are the product's storefront. They decide 90% of a video's reach. This operationalizes the Brand Bible's Sprint 2 (thumbnails) and Sprint 3 (creative intelligence) into a repeatable packaging routine.

## The non-negotiable: three concepts, every time

Never produce one title/thumbnail pair. Always build three, deliberately different, then score and choose:

- **Concept 1 — the safe read.** The clearest expression of the promise. (Your [[Video-Backlog]] title is usually this.)
- **Concept 2 — the tension play.** Leads with the counterintuitive angle. Higher curiosity, higher risk.
- **Concept 3 — the wildcard.** Different template, different emotional angle. Breaks the pattern on purpose.

Building three forces range and stops you settling for the first (usually most generic) idea.

## Score before you publish

Rate each concept 1–5 on four axes. **A concept scoring below 3 on Payoff honesty is rejected outright, regardless of other scores** — clickbait the video can't cash poisons retention and kills the channel slowly (brand rule #5).

| Axis | Question |
|---|---|
| Clarity at glance | Legible promise in under 1 second at mobile size? |
| Tension | Does it open a genuine curiosity gap? |
| Fit | Looks/sounds like *this* channel and no other? |
| Payoff honesty | Can the video actually deliver this? *(hard gate)* |

Pick the highest total that clears the honesty gate. When two are close, choose the one that looks **more like the brand**, not more like everyone else.

## Title patterns (the headline engine)

Every title is built from a structure, not improvised. Front-load the strongest word (mobile truncates ~first 3 words), one idea per title, 40–55 characters.

| Structure | Skeleton | Example |
|---|---|---|
| The mechanism | "How [outcome] actually happens" | How power moves in a room nobody's leading |
| The reframe | "[Belief] is wrong. Here's what's real." | Hard work isn't leverage. This is. |
| The named law | "The [name] trap / rule / effect" | The competence trap: why the best gets stuck |
| The insider | "What [group] knows that you don't" | What operators understand about timing |
| Stakes, answered | "Why [surprising outcome]" | Why the loudest person loses the long game |
| The Machiavelli read | apply a line from *The Prince* | Machiavelli said be feared. He was half right. |

Use the Machiavelli structure roughly **one title in three** — enough to keep the anchor visible, not so much it's the only note.

## Thumbnail templates (drop content in, don't design from scratch)

Five locked templates. Every video maps to one. Build them once as reusable files.

- **A — Portrait / power:** subject face upper-left, gold hook word right. *For a person or archetype.*
- **B — Object / symbol:** single symbol center-left on black, text stacked right. *For concept videos.*
- **C — Versus:** two subjects/words split by a thin gold line. *For comparison/conflict.*
- **D — Text-dominant:** one short brutal statement, gold key word. *For big-idea/list videos.*
- **E — Scene:** an environment (boardroom, chessboard, throne) with a small text block. *For narrative/case studies.*

**Signature language (must be present):** near-black background, single hard light source, muted gold used *sparingly* (one word/line/glint — it's the fingerprint), steel-blue rim light, monospace left-aligned text, **one idea per thumbnail**. The channel should be identifiable with the logo removed.

## The symbol library

One symbol per thumbnail, in the light-and-shadow style, when a literal image would be weaker:

chess piece (strategy) · **fox & lion** (the signature — cunning vs. force, use at the anchor's heart) · the worn book (*The Prince* itself) · chains / broken chain (control & its loss) · mask (presented self vs. real motive) · crown / empty throne (power, succession) · puppet strings (influence) · spotlight in darkness (being seen, being read).

## Safe zones (or it fails on mobile)

- Keep the **bottom-right 220×90px clear** — YouTube stamps the duration there.
- Nothing critical within **40px of any edge** (mobile crop + UI).
- Faces: eyes on the upper-third line, never cropped by the timestamp.
- **Test at 210×118px** (mobile feed size). If the words vanish, the thumbnail failed — remake it.
