# YouTube Trend Briefing — 2026-07-10

YouTube Shorts drops the dislike button, adds 2x speed and Clear Screen mode

What shipped: YouTube rolled out a Shorts player redesign this week (confirmed via a Creator Insider video on July 3) that removes the visible dislike count in favor of a heart icon, adds a 2x playback speed option, a mute toggle, and a Clear Screen mode that hides all on-screen text and icons.

Why it matters: For a beginner faceless channel leaning on Shorts, viewer behavior signals are shifting — swipe-away speed and "not interested" taps matter more than dislikes now, so your main optimization target should be killing the urge to swipe in the first second, not chasing likes.

YouTube Studio replaces the Monetization and Restrictions columns with a single color-coded "Notices" system

What shipped: YouTube redesigned the Content tab in Studio, consolidating monetization and restriction flags into one red/yellow/gray "Notices" column and adding a new per-video Estimated Revenue column covering ads, Premium, Shopping, and fan funding.

Why it matters: This gives a beginner channel owner a much faster way to see exactly which videos are demonetized or limited and why, instead of digging through separate tabs — useful for catching an inauthentic-content flag on one video before it drags down the whole channel.

Faceless niche rankings for July 2026: finance, true crime, and AI tutorials lead

What shipped: Fresh July niche-performance data ranks finance/wealth explainers, true-crime narration and animated horror, and AI tool tutorials as the top-earning and fastest-growing faceless categories, with finance RPMs still running $15-30+ and AI tutorial demand outpacing the supply of creators covering it.

Why it matters: A beginner picking a niche right now has a clearer signal to commit to one of these three lanes rather than guessing, since each combines above-average ad rates with proven, growing viewer demand.
